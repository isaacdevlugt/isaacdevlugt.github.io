Isaac De Vlugt

Candidate for MSc Physics, University of Waterloo, Waterloo, ON

I study condensed matter physics under the supervision of Roger Melko (group website), where we employ state of the art computational techniques to study quantum matter.

My CV can be found here.

Current Research

Currently, my main interests are in neural network expressiveness, modifying network architectures for more suitable application to real problems and experiments, and why / how generative models are able to reconstruct properties of quantum states better than others. Beyond machine learning, I work on developing quantum Monte Carlo techniques to facilitate the exploration of new phases of matter.

Papers

My publications involve open-source software for quantum state tomography using neural networks, RBMs for state reconstruction in exotic Hilbert spaces and ab. initio studies on transition-metal boron-halide/fluoride structures. Check out my google scholar to learn more.
